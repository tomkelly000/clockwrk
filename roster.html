Bio: The ongoing story of Anthony Markowitz is one of subtle ruthlessness and terrifying power. Anthony's tenure on Clockwork started innocently enough – at least to untrained eyes. After joining, Anthony quietly accepted a day job as a Clockwork B-Team cutter. He silently went about his work, and did not draw any undue attention to himself. He seemed like an ordinary engineer – we had no cause for suspicion and no forewarning of what was to come. Before long, however, Anthony made his first move, claiming the job of B-Team Frosh Captain. He seemed to accept his role under upperclass captain Adam Smyles humbly enough, but before long, Smyles started missing practices. He would seem to vanish totally from the earth, only to reappear after all important administrative decisions were made by the remaining captains. We suspected nothing, dismissing it as “Classic Smyles.” However, by the time Georgia rolled around, ClockBlock's other upperclass captain suffered mysterious burns to his legs, rendering him totally unable to play. Decision-making power fell to Anthony. We didn't suspect anything yet, but that was before we knew the truth. In actuality, Anthony was a sorcerer of terrible power. He began studying magic at a very young age, and his dabbling in the darkest arts quickly grew to an obsession. He would practice his craft in secret for hours every day, preparing for the day when the moment would arise to seize power and dominate the world. Smyles was Anthony's first true victim. While Smyles was on his way to practice, Anthony would teleport him into an alternate dimension (codename “Philadelphia”), only to pull him back out after Anthony had his way in the captains' meetings. Andy Zhu was his second. We later recovered small pieces of playing cards from deep within Andy's charred legs – a calling card that Anthony would soon become infamous for. After seizing control of the B-Team, Anthony quickly set his sights on loftier goals. Over the summer, he isolated himself on a mysterious and somewhat shady island of Jew Nersey and consulted with the Hindu god Krishna. Before long, Anthony was deeply immersed an art so dark that is has no speakable name – among Clockwork players, we refer to it with fear as V-J-B. With this new art, Anthony hypnotized the entirety of Clockwork, distracting them with his newly-jacked thighs as he wrenched the presidency from Jae Lee, who mysteriously broke his foot “playing basketball.” Anthony asserted his complete dominance, ruthlessly accumulating power and extorting money from Clockwork's pockets to fund his ever-growing obsession with sorcery. Happily, however, Anthony's rule as president of Clockwork has caused him to put his plans for world domination on hold in favor of dominating the world of Ultimate. He is known to make discs disappear completely and reappear in the hands of cutters in the endzone, and he frequently takes control of opposing player's minds to force them to drop the disc. Stephen Wang claims that such magic is the reason he threw three Callahans in a row in practice, but Anthony claims that it's just because Wang sucks. Anthony is looking forward to killing it this year with his magical hucks, sleight-of-hand defenive measures, and ruthless use of mind control. Today, he dominates ultimate....tomorrow, he'll dominate the world.

Stephen Wang

AKA Wang

5'10''

Class of 2014

Hometown: Baton Rouge, LA

Club teams: Gunswick

Bio: A highly sought after recruit from the swamps of Louisiana, Stephen Wang had ultimate scouts drooling over his potential. Affectionately known as "Every Coach's Dream", Wang had a cannon of an arm, pogo sticks for legs, and the entire country in anticipation about his impending college decision. On a nationally televised special Wang announced that he would be taking his talents to New Jersey to unite with hometown hero Andrew Bergman and fellow prized recruit Neal Donnelly, netting himself an army of haters at home and the Boys and Girls Club of Louisiana about $2 million.
However the "Wang Dynasty" didn't begin as successfully as expected as Wang struggled to carry his teammates and the country's expectations. Additionally, disaster struck for Stephen both personally and professionally. A severe case of eye herpes plagued him on the morning of a tournament and severely limited his ability, inevitably leading to a disaster now known as the "Massacre in Mercer" where Clockwork almost managed to lose to its own B-team. However, the worst was yet to come, as Clockwork Ultimate was about to experience the biggest sports scandal this side of Tiger Woods. First a few isolated rumors from TMZ began filtering in and then the bombshell came from a most curious of sources. A mysterious insider, who only identified herself as VQu to protect her identity, informed ESPN via Skype that the rumors were true: Wang indeed had a tiny penis.
Wang was devastated. After a brief layoff from college ultimate for personal reasons, Wang rededicated himself to the game he loves. From hitting the weight room at the crack of dawn to sprinting to the farthest poles he could find, Wang was determined to compensate for his now famous deficiency with tremendous on-field success, and the results were staggering. His sheer determination almost carried Clockwork to a surprise bid to Nationals in his sophomore season.
It's interesting to think about how Ultivillage historians centuries from now will classify Stephen's time at Princeton. The next two years will ultimately determine whether the Wang Dynasty was a rousing success in the face of adversity or an experiment derailed by scandal. Despite all this uncertainty, however, one thing is for sure: nobody will be working harder than Wang.
